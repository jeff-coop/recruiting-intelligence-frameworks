# Closing Framework

This page documents how I think about closing candidates and managing offers.

Closing is often viewed as something that happens at the end of the hiring process.

In reality, closing starts with the first conversation.

The goal is not to convince someone to accept an offer.

The goal is to understand motivations, build trust, identify concerns early, and help candidates make informed decisions.

---

# First Principle

Closing Starts Early

The strongest closes rarely happen during the offer stage.

They happen because the recruiter has spent the entire process understanding:

* Motivations
* Career Goals
* Priorities
* Concerns
* Decision Criteria

By the time an offer arrives, there should be very few surprises.

---

# Understanding Motivation

One of the first things I try to understand is:

Why is this person considering a move?

Examples:

* Career Growth
* Leadership Opportunities
* Compensation
* Technology
* Impact
* Stability
* Learning Opportunities

Understanding motivation helps guide the entire process.

---

# Identifying Risks Early

Questions:

* What concerns do they have?
* What would prevent them from accepting?
* What alternatives are they considering?
* What needs to be true for this opportunity to make sense?

The earlier risks are identified, the easier they are to address.

---

# Understanding The Candidate's Process

Questions:

* Are they interviewing elsewhere?
* How far along are they?
* Are there competing offers?
* What is their timeline?

This information helps set expectations and avoid surprises.

---

# Maintaining Alignment

Throughout the process I try to consistently revisit:

* Interest Level
* Concerns
* Expectations
* Compensation

Alignment should be ongoing.

Not a single conversation before an offer.

---

# The Offer Stage

At the offer stage, clarity becomes critical.

Topics:

* Compensation
* Bonus
* Equity
* Benefits
* Career Growth
* Team Structure

Candidates should understand the full opportunity.

Not just the base salary.

---

# Handling Counteroffers

Counteroffers are common.

Questions:

* Why were they looking initially?
* Has anything fundamentally changed?
* Which concerns are actually being solved?

Many counteroffers address symptoms rather than root causes.

---

# Common Closing Mistakes

Examples:

* Discussing compensation too late
* Ignoring concerns
* Assuming interest
* Not understanding competing processes
* Waiting until the offer stage to start closing

Most closing challenges appear much earlier in the process.

---

# Candidate Advocacy

Strong recruiters advocate for both sides.

Examples:

* Providing candidate context
* Sharing concerns with hiring teams
* Helping candidates evaluate opportunities
* Managing expectations

Trust is built through transparency.

---

# Questions I'm Trying To Answer

Throughout a process, I am usually asking:

* Why does this opportunity matter to this person?
* What concerns exist?
* What alternatives are being considered?
* What factors will drive a decision?
* How confident am I in an acceptance?

---

# Final Observation

Closing is not about pressure.

Closing is about understanding.

The better I understand a candidate's motivations, priorities, and concerns, the more likely both sides are to reach the right decision.
