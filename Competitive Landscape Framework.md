# Competitive Landscape Framework

This page documents how I think about competitive talent landscapes when supporting hiring efforts.

The goal is not to create a list of competitor companies.

The goal is to understand where talent exists, how it moves, and which organizations consistently develop strong technical talent.

Understanding the competitive landscape helps improve recruiting strategy, market mapping, and talent intelligence efforts.

---

# What Is Competitive Landscape Analysis?

Competitive landscape analysis is the process of understanding:

* Talent Density
* Talent Movement
* Hiring Patterns
* Company Ecosystems
* Skill Concentrations

The objective is to understand the market before entering it.

---

# Step 1: Define The Talent

Before identifying competitors, I define the talent itself.

Questions:

* What problems does this person solve?
* What skills matter most?
* What environments create this talent?

Examples:

Infrastructure Engineers

* Distributed Systems
* Databases
* Cloud Infrastructure
* Reliability

AI Engineers

* Machine Learning
* Research
* AI Infrastructure

---

# Step 2: Identify Core Competitors

These are organizations hiring similar talent.

Examples:

Infrastructure

* Databricks
* Snowflake
* MongoDB
* Confluent
* Datadog

AI

* OpenAI
* Anthropic
* Cohere
* Scale AI

---

# Step 3: Identify Adjacent Competitors

Many strong candidates come from adjacent markets.

Examples:

Infrastructure

* Platform Engineering
* Developer Productivity
* Site Reliability Engineering

AI

* Data Engineering
* Research Engineering
* Applied Science

---

# Step 4: Understand Talent Density

Questions:

* Which companies have the highest concentration of talent?
* Which organizations consistently produce strong candidates?
* Which teams are known for technical depth?

---

# Step 5: Study Talent Movement

Questions:

* Where do candidates come from?
* Where do they go?
* Which transitions occur frequently?

Patterns often reveal hidden talent pools.

---

# Step 6: Evaluate Ecosystems

Strong talent ecosystems often include:

* Universities
* Research Labs
* Open Source Communities
* Conferences
* Fellowship Programs

Companies rarely create talent alone.

They usually exist within larger ecosystems.

---

# Questions I'm Trying To Answer

* Where does talent develop?
* Which companies produce strong candidates?
* Which adjacent pools exist?
* Where is future talent likely to emerge?
* What signals appear repeatedly?

---

# Final Observation

The best talent maps rarely start with people.

They start with ecosystems.

The better I understand the ecosystem, the better I understand the talent.
