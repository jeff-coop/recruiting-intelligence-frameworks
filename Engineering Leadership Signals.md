# Engineering Leadership Signals

This page documents how I think about engineering leadership when evaluating technical talent.

The goal is not to identify managers.

The goal is to identify leadership.

Leadership can exist at every level of an organization.

Many exceptional engineers demonstrate leadership long before receiving a management title.

---

# First Principle

Leadership and management are not the same thing.

Management is a role.

Leadership is influence.

Strong leaders often create leverage for others.

---

# Technical Leadership

Examples:

* Driving architecture decisions
* Leading migrations
* Defining technical strategy
* Improving engineering standards
* Mentoring engineers

Questions:

* Do others rely on them?
* Do they influence decisions?
* Do they improve technical outcomes?

---

# Ownership

One of the strongest leadership signals.

Examples:

* Building new systems
* Solving ambiguous problems
* Leading major initiatives
* Improving reliability
* Improving developer productivity

Ownership often appears before formal leadership.

---

# Mentorship

Examples:

* Onboarding Engineers
* Career Development
* Technical Coaching
* Knowledge Sharing

Strong leaders often help others succeed.

---

# Communication

Examples:

* Technical Writing
* Design Documents
* Presentations
* Cross Functional Collaboration

Questions:

* Can they communicate complexity clearly?
* Can they influence without authority?

---

# Decision Making

Leadership often requires making decisions with incomplete information.

Examples:

* Architectural Tradeoffs
* Technical Prioritization
* Risk Assessment

Strong leaders demonstrate judgment.

---

# Organizational Impact

Questions:

* Did their work influence a team?
* A department?
* An organization?

The broader the impact, the stronger the signal.

---

# Signals I Find Most Interesting

Examples:

* Technical Leadership
* Mentorship
* Ownership
* Cross Functional Influence
* Strategic Thinking
* Communication
* Organizational Impact

---

# Signals I Care Less About

Examples:

* Management Title Alone
* Team Size Alone
* Years Of Experience Alone

Leadership requires context.

---

# Questions I'm Trying To Answer

* Who do others trust?
* Who creates leverage?
* Who improves systems?
* Who helps teams succeed?
* Who influences outcomes?

---

# Final Observation

Leadership is often visible long before a title appears on a resume.

Many exceptional leaders begin by consistently creating value for others.
