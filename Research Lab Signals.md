# Research Lab Signals

This page documents how I think about research labs when evaluating technical talent.

The goal is not to rank institutions or academic programs.

The goal is to understand the environments where technical talent develops and the experiences research labs may provide.

Research labs often sit at the intersection of curiosity, experimentation, and technical depth.

---

# First Principle

Research labs are environments for learning.

The most valuable signal is often not the lab itself, but what someone did while they were there.

Questions:

* What problems were they working on?
* What was their contribution?
* What skills did they develop?

---

# Common Research Areas

Examples:

* Artificial Intelligence
* Machine Learning
* Distributed Systems
* Databases
* Security
* Networking
* Robotics
* Human Computer Interaction

Different research areas often develop different skill sets.

---

# Signals I Find Most Interesting

Examples:

* Long-Term Research Projects
* Publications
* Open Source Contributions
* Research Engineering
* Technical Writing
* Independent Research

These often indicate curiosity and persistence.

---

# Research Engineering

One of the strongest signals.

Questions:

* Did they build systems?
* Did they support experiments?
* Did they bridge research and implementation?

Research engineering often combines theory and practical application.

---

# Collaboration

Research is rarely done alone.

Examples:

* Working with professors
* Working with graduate students
* Cross-functional collaboration
* Multi-disciplinary projects

Strong collaboration often appears alongside strong technical ability.

---

# Questions I'm Trying To Answer

* What motivated this work?
* What technical challenges were involved?
* What was their contribution?
* What did they learn?
* How did they grow?

---

# Final Observation

Research labs often expose people to difficult problems long before they encounter them in industry.

That experience can create strong foundations for future growth.
