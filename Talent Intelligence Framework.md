# Talent Intelligence Framework

This page documents how I think about talent intelligence and the role it plays in recruiting, workforce planning, and talent strategy.

The goal of talent intelligence is not simply to find candidates.

The goal is to understand talent markets, identify patterns, anticipate challenges, and help organizations make better hiring decisions.

Talent intelligence sits at the intersection of recruiting, research, market analysis, workforce planning, and business strategy.

---

# What Is Talent Intelligence?

Talent intelligence is the process of collecting, organizing, analyzing, and applying talent market information to support hiring and workforce decisions.

Examples:

* Talent Availability
* Talent Density
* Compensation Trends
* Competitor Hiring Activity
* Geographic Analysis
* Skill Supply

The objective is better decision making.

---

# Core Principle

Recruiting answers:

"Who should we hire?"

Talent intelligence helps answer:

"Where should we look?"

"What should we expect?"

"What challenges exist?"

"What opportunities exist?"

---

# Pillar 1: Market Understanding

Before solving a hiring problem, I want to understand the market.

Questions:

* How large is the talent pool?
* Which companies employ this talent?
* Which locations have talent density?
* How competitive is the market?

---

# Pillar 2: Ecosystem Analysis

Talent rarely develops in isolation.

Strong ecosystems often include:

* Universities
* Research Labs
* Companies
* Open Source Communities
* Conferences
* Professional Networks

Understanding the ecosystem often reveals talent before traditional sourcing does.

---

# Pillar 3: Talent Signals

I try to identify signals that may indicate technical depth, growth, curiosity, and potential.

Examples:

* Open Source
* Research
* Technical Writing
* Leadership
* Teaching
* Community Participation

Signals provide context beyond resumes and job titles.

---

# Pillar 4: Competitive Intelligence

Understanding competitors helps improve hiring strategy.

Questions:

* Which companies hire similar talent?
* Which companies produce strong candidates?
* What backgrounds appear repeatedly?

The goal is understanding patterns, not copying competitors.

---

# Pillar 5: Future Talent

Some of the best talent is not yet in the market.

Examples:

* Students
* Interns
* Researchers
* Fellowship Participants
* Open Source Contributors

Talent intelligence should identify future talent, not just current talent.

# Questions I Frequently Explore

Examples:

* Which companies produce the strongest infrastructure engineers?
* Which universities have strong AI programs?
* Which open source communities create technical depth?

---

# Common Mistakes

Examples:

* Focusing only on current openings
* Ignoring future talent pipelines
* Overvaluing company logos
* Ignoring adjacent talent pools
* Treating talent intelligence as sourcing

Talent intelligence should influence strategy, not just execution.
