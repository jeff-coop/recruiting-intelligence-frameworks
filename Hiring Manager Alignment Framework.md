# Hiring Manager Alignment Framework

This page documents how I think about hiring manager alignment during a search.

Many recruiting challenges are not sourcing problems.

They are alignment problems.

The goal is to create a shared understanding of what success looks like before candidates enter the process.

Strong alignment improves candidate quality, hiring velocity, and overall hiring outcomes.

---

# First Principle

The job description is rarely enough.

Before beginning a search, I want to understand:

* Why does this role exist?
* What problem does this person solve?
* What does success look like after 6 months?
* What does success look like after 12 months?

The best searches start with clarity.

---

# Understanding The Business Need

Questions I typically ask:

* Why is this position open?
* Is it backfill or growth?
* What are the team's priorities?
* What challenges is the team facing?

Understanding the business context often changes how I approach the search.

---

# Defining The Must-Haves

One of the most important conversations.

Questions:

* What skills are truly required?
* What skills can be learned?
* What backgrounds have worked previously?
* What backgrounds have not worked?

The goal is to separate preferences from requirements.

---

# Understanding The Team

Questions:

* Who will this person work with?
* What is the team structure?
* What technical challenges exist?
* What gaps currently exist?

Strong candidates often complement teams rather than duplicate them.

---

# Building Candidate Profiles

I like to create examples.

Questions:

* What companies should we target?
* What adjacent companies should we consider?
* What backgrounds transfer well?
* What unexpected talent pools may exist?

Examples help create alignment quickly.

---

# Understanding Compensation

Questions:

* What is the budget?
* How flexible is the range?
* How competitive is the package?

Misalignment here can create unnecessary delays later.

---

# Defining The Interview Process

Before sourcing begins, I want to understand:

* Interview stages
* Decision makers
* Evaluation criteria
* Timeline

Candidates value clarity.

Recruiters need predictability.

---

# Calibration Early

One of the biggest mistakes in recruiting is waiting too long to calibrate.

After the first few interviews, I usually ask:

* What did we like?
* What did we dislike?
* What patterns are emerging?
* Should we adjust the search?

Early feedback prevents wasted effort.

---

# Common Misalignment Risks

Examples:

* Unrealistic Expectations
* Too Many Requirements
* Lack Of Interview Consistency
* Unclear Evaluation Criteria
* Market Misunderstanding

Most searches benefit from continuous calibration.

---

# Questions I'm Trying To Answer

Before launching a search, I am usually asking:

* What problem are we solving?
* What does success look like?
* What backgrounds are most relevant?
* What tradeoffs are acceptable?
* How will candidates be evaluated?

---

# Final Observation

The strongest recruiting partnerships are built on alignment.

When recruiters and hiring managers share the same understanding of success, the search becomes significantly more effective.
