# Internship Signals

This page documents how I think about internships when evaluating early-career technical talent.

The goal is not to rank internship programs.

The goal is to understand what experiences internships may provide and what signals they can reveal about a candidate's development, interests, and future potential.

Internships are often one of the first opportunities students have to apply technical skills in a real-world environment.

---

# First Principle

An internship is an experience, not an outcome.

The company name alone rarely tells the full story.

Questions:

* What did the candidate work on?
* What did they learn?
* What level of ownership did they have?
* What impact did they make?

The experience matters more than the brand.

---

# What Internships Can Signal

Examples:

* Technical Growth
* Curiosity
* Learning Velocity
* Professional Development
* Collaboration
* Communication

Internships often provide a glimpse into how someone learns in a professional environment.

---

# Ownership

One of the strongest signals.

Examples:

* Built Features
* Shipped Projects
* Improved Systems
* Solved Problems Independently

Questions:

* Were they trusted with meaningful work?
* Did they take initiative?

---

# Technical Depth

Examples:

* Infrastructure Projects
* AI Projects
* Data Engineering
* Backend Development
* Platform Engineering

Questions:

* What technical challenges did they encounter?
* How deeply did they engage with the work?

---

# Learning Velocity

Internships are often short.

The ability to learn quickly becomes important.

Examples:

* New Technologies
* New Codebases
* New Teams
* New Domains

Strong candidates often ramp quickly.

---

# Communication

Examples:

* Presentations
* Documentation
* Team Collaboration
* Cross-Functional Work

Many internship programs evaluate communication just as heavily as technical ability.

---

# Multiple Internships

Multiple internships can provide useful context.

Questions:

* Is there progression?
* Are responsibilities increasing?
* Is a pattern emerging?

Examples:

* Startup → Growth Company
* Startup → Big Tech
* Research → Industry

Patterns often reveal interests and growth.

---

# Internship Ecosystems

Certain organizations consistently invest in intern development.

Examples:

* Technology Companies
* Quantitative Trading Firms
* Research Organizations
* Defense Technology Companies
* Infrastructure Companies

The strongest programs often provide:

* Mentorship
* Ownership
* Exposure To Complex Problems

---

# Signals I Find Most Interesting

Examples:

* Meaningful Project Ownership
* Technical Depth
* Strong Mentorship Environments
* Return Offers
* Progressive Internship Experience
* Research + Industry Experience

---

# Signals I Care Less About

Examples:

* Company Name Alone
* Internship Count Alone
* Prestige Alone

Without context, these signals are often incomplete.

---

# Questions I'm Trying To Answer

When reviewing internship experience, I am usually asking:

* What did this person learn?
* What did they build?
* What responsibilities did they have?
* How did they grow?
* What signals suggest future potential?

---

# Final Observation

Great internships rarely create great engineers.

They accelerate engineers who are already curious, motivated, and eager to learn.

The strongest signal is often what someone chooses to do with the opportunity once they have it.
